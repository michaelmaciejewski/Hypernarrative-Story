Coors Field

Getting off the exit into Downtown Denver, the Coors Field offers a beautiful entrance, sitting beneath the rest of the city. Although I have not been to that many games, the Colorado Rockies are a popular team around town. The field is known to baseball fans as a "hitters park", being that the altitude affects the distance of the ball. I take note of the landscape which reflects the Rocky Mountain elements and embrace the tone of purple which spreads across the building. An interesting discovery during the construction include findings of dinasaur fossils including skulls. The stadium is a hallmark to the city and I can feel the joyus atmosphere surrounding.
